illeat | Mobile App Design Process

illeat is a local service that provides curated, low-cost recipes based on weekly deals and promotions at registered grocery stores in town.
The idea was originally invented to save “broke and lazy” students who usually have no idea what items they should buy at the grocery store even when those are on sale.
This case study shows the process of building the illeat mobile app, from creating concept to designing a prototype.

Process

Problem Identification

It is noticed that some shoppers tend to feel clueless about what meal they should cook for a day. They are mostly people who want to save money on food but not sure what recipes fit their budget. Besides, as they are busy, they want to save time on cooking and browsing ingredients at the store. This research below is therefore conducted to assess this issue.

User Research

The main objective of this research is to understand the insights into people’s grocery shopping habits then identify their pain points and specific problems during their experience. Research interviews* are conducted on 3 subjects in the target audience pool (students and people with jobs). 03 empathy maps are then created based on the interview findings, and problem statement is discovered.

Problem Statement

Busy people who wants to commit to their fitness and diet routine need a way to remind, motivate and track how they are progressing toward their goals because they feel like they lack self-control and disciplines

Market Research

Through some research, it is discovered that Flipp, BBC Good Food and BigOven are current popular apps that may solve half of the problem above.
Though these 3 apps have their own strengths and drawbacks to the user’s problem, it would be more simple and convenient for the shoppers if these functions are combined into a seamless app that offers recipes, list of low-cost ingredients and where to find them.

Brand Buiding

Logo Creation

Inspired by chopsticks and chopsticks holder in Asian cuisine, the logotype embodies their minimal shapes as a stylized pair of letter L and the apostrophe.
Many logo types was explored to find the best typeface. The chosen one is a rounded typeface whose softness tends to make them feel friendly and inviting.
A stand-alone version of the logo is created to use as a mobile application icon.

Color Palette

The palette consists of Red as the primary one as it is the color that also triggers appetite and is used on a majority of food packaging design.Other supplement colors are extracted from real life minimal images.

Typeface

Matias and Brevia Light make good contrast in visualization. Matias is chosen to be in the logotype as it represents the amiable, easy-going feeling, while Brevia Light is elegant, modern and readable.

Business Card

Business Model

From business perspective: Illeat is a white-label SaaS that aims to help small and medium grocery stores to reach their customers through content strategy with a starting database of 1000 recipes and more to be updated.

From user's perspective: illeat is built as an application that provides curated, low-cost recipes based on weekly deals at registered grocery stores to serve the needs of busy, low and medium income users.
