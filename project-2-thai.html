Northern Thai | Website Redesign Process

Northern Thai is a restaurant in downtown Kitchener and is in desperate need of a new brand and website. This case study shows the process of re-designing Northern Thai new brand guideline and website.

Type

Personal project

Role

Solo UX Designer

Length

1 week

Tasks

Design logo, color palette, typefaces, and website

Tools

Photoshop, Illustrator, Figma

Objective

Create a new branding package for Northern Thai

Northern Thai’s Current Website

The current Northern Thai website has these following issues that need to be fixed:
• No brand logo, the current one is just a generic symbol of Thailand.
• Inconsistent typefaces and fonts.
• No call-to-action section (interactive).
• Blur, low-quality image.
• About us: Flash gallery fails to run, description about the restaurant is super hard to read.

Brand Buiding

Logo Creation

Inspired by iconic Thailand views and cultures, the color palette is a combinaton of warm shades of red, orange and yellow. Red symbolizes hot chilli, orange represents curries and yellow is found in turmeric or curcumin, popular spices in Thai food.

Color Palette

The palette consists of Red as the primary one as it is the color that also triggers appetite and is used on a majority of food packaging design.Other supplement colors are extracted from real life minimal images.

Typeface

Matias and Brevia Light make good contrast in visualization. Matias represents the amiable and casual feeling, while Brevia Light is elegant, modern and readable. This combination makes Northern Thai look more modern and friendly to customers.

Website Redesign Mockup

The website is re-designed with warm tones and shades as these colors symbolize spices and flavors of Thai foods, also remains the yellow shade of the original website.
The idea is to connect the past and present as Northern Thai has been serving their customers since 1997; for instance, the About section therefore, consists of a black and white photo as a flashback to the history of the restaurant.
